"You're such a cynic."

Erika shrugged. "If you say so. But it's not so bad. I'm fucked up, but so is everybody else. Knowing makes it easier to relate to them."

"Empathize, maybe even?" Sebastian asked, raising an eyebrow. 

"Maybe," she said, scowling. But only for a moment.

Sebastian settled back into the bed, pulling the blankets over his shoulders. The moonlight reflecting off the snow cast a dim blue light through their cabin, making all surfaces seem ghostly and soft, save where shadows pooled. Sebastian's silhouette was mottled with them. Beneath his brow, in the folds of his blankets, even in the rough skin of his scars. But what was free of them seemed to glow with its own light.
