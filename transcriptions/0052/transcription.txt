"Sasha?" Sebastian asked. His voice creaked out, refusing to be pushed into use.

"Not quite," Sebastian heard the figure say. It walked towards him, wearing Sasha's face and frame, but the way it carried itself was wrong. Too smooth and too confidently. 

"Who are you?" Sebastian's voice stammared as he backed himself against the gate. He slipped backwards, brusing knees and elbows on the rough tstone. The man paused. 

"I am the dreamwalker," he said. "And try and wak yourself, just a bit. It's very difficult to have a conversation with you when you're so far into the dream."

His words were distant and seemed to arrive slowly. Sebastian heard him as though they were spoken through molten glass. He pieced them together slowly.
